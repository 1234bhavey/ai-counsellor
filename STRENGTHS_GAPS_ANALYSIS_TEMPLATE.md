# Strengths & Gaps Analysis Template

## Template Structure

**Analyze the student's profile.**

**Profile Data:**
{{profile_data}}

**Output format:**

**Strengths:**
- (bullet points)

**Gaps / Risks:**
- (bullet points)

**Overall Assessment:**
- 2 clear lines

**Rules:**
- Be honest and realistic
- Do NOT recommend universities yet

## Analysis Framework

### Strengths Categories

#### Academic Strengths
- Strong academic performance with excellent CGPA (3.7+)
- Solid academic foundation with competitive CGPA (3.3-3.6)
- Clear academic progression path with defined objectives

#### Test Preparation Strengths
- English proficiency requirement fulfilled (IELTS completed)
- Standardized test requirement completed (GRE/GMAT done)
- Proactive test scheduling shows good planning

#### Planning & Preparation Strengths
- Financial planning completed with realistic budget
- Timeline management demonstrates organization
- Research and preparation show commitment

### Gaps & Risks Categories

#### Academic Gaps
- Low CGPA significantly limits admission chances (<3.0)
- Below-average CGPA restricts top-tier options (3.0-3.3)

#### Test Preparation Gaps
- Critical gap: No standardized tests initiated
- IELTS preparation not started - proficiency bottleneck
- GRE preparation missing for target programs

#### Financial Gaps
- Insufficient budget for target destination
- Financial planning incomplete - strategy uncertainty

#### Timeline Risks
- Compressed preparation timeline affects quality
- Concurrent test prep may dilute focus
- Late start creates deadline pressure

### Overall Assessment Guidelines

#### High Readiness (7+ points)
- Line 1: "Strong readiness with solid foundation and preparation"
- Line 2: "Well-positioned to proceed with university research"

#### Moderate Readiness (4-6 points)
- Line 1: "Moderate readiness with gaps requiring attention"
- Line 2: "Focus on completing requirements to strengthen candidacy"

#### Low Readiness (0-3 points)
- Line 1: "Early-stage preparation with significant gaps"
- Line 2: "Comprehensive preparation plan needed before proceeding"

## Example Analyses

### Example 1: Strong Profile
```
**Strengths:**
- Strong academic performance with excellent CGPA demonstrates consistent high achievement
- English proficiency requirement fulfilled - removes major application barrier
- Financial planning completed with realistic budget assessment for international education

**Gaps / Risks:**
- GRE preparation not initiated - required for most graduate programs, especially in competitive fields

**Overall Assessment:**
- Profile demonstrates strong readiness for international graduate applications with solid academic foundation and test preparation.
- Candidate is well-positioned to proceed with university research and application strategy development.
```

### Example 2: Moderate Profile
```
**Strengths:**
- Solid academic foundation with competitive CGPA for most programs
- Proactive test scheduling shows good timeline management and planning

**Gaps / Risks:**
- IELTS preparation not started - English proficiency requirement creates application bottleneck
- Exam preparation timeline appears compressed - risk of inadequate preparation affecting scores

**Overall Assessment:**
- Profile shows moderate readiness with some strong elements but requires attention to identified gaps before applications.
- Focus on completing pending requirements and addressing risk factors to strengthen overall candidacy.
```

### Example 3: Developing Profile
```
**Strengths:**
- Clear academic progression path with defined educational objectives

**Gaps / Risks:**
- Low CGPA significantly limits admission chances at competitive institutions and scholarship opportunities
- Critical gap: No standardized test preparation initiated - severe timeline risk for upcoming deadlines

```

## Integration with AI Counsellor

This template is integrated into the AI Counsellor's Stage 2 (Profile Analysis) responses. The system will:

1. **Analyze profile data** using the strengths/gaps framework
2. **Provide honest, realistic assessment** without sugar-coating issues
3. **Identify specific strengths** to build confidence
4. **Highlight critical gaps** that need attention
5. **Give clear overall assessment** in exactly 2 lines
6. **Maintain stage discipline** - no university recommendations

### Key Principles

- **Honesty over encouragement** - realistic assessment helps students
- **Specific over generic** - detailed analysis more valuable than platitudes
- **Actionable insights** - gaps should suggest clear next steps
- **Stage compliance** - analysis only, no premature recommendations
- **Professional tone** - supportive but objective evaluation

This format provides structured, professional analysis that helps students understand their current position without jumping ahead to university recommendations.
